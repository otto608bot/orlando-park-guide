Magic Kingdom: Perfect for Little Ones

Magic Kingdom is the ultimate destination for toddlers. More than half the rides here are designed with young children in mind.

"it's a small world"

The quintessential toddler ride. Gentle boat cruise through rooms filled with singing dolls from around the world. The song will be stuck in your head for days, but the look of wonder on your toddler's face is worth it. Height requirement: None

The Many Adventures of Winnie the Pooh

Hop into a honey pot and journey through the Hundred Acre Wood. Bright colors, familiar characters, and a gentle pace make this a toddler favorite. Height requirement: None

Peter Pan's Flight

Soar over London in a pirate ship and through Neverland. The flying sensation captivates toddlers without being scary. Parent tip: Use Genie+ for this oneâ€”lines get long fast.

Dumbo the Flying Elephant

A classic for a reason. Toddlers love controlling how high their elephant flies. The indoor play area while you wait is a lifesaver for wiggly little ones. Height requirement: None

Mad Tea Party

Spin in a giant teacupâ€”you control the speed. Toddlers love the colors and the gentle spinning (just don't overdo it!). Height requirement: None

Under the Sea ~ Journey of The Little Mermaid

Clam-shell vehicles take you through Ariel's world with all the classic songs. Entirely smooth with no drops or surprises. Height requirement: None

Disney's Animal Kingdom: Wild and Gentle

Na'vi River Journey

A peaceful boat ride through the bioluminescent forests of Pandora. The glowing plants and gentle music mesmerize toddlers. Height requirement: None

Kilimanjaro Safaris

See real animals from an open-air vehicle. Toddlers love spotting giraffes, elephants, and lions. Can be bumpyâ€”hold tight to little ones. Height requirement: None

TriceraTop Spin

Like Dumbo, but with dinosaurs. Toddlers control how high their triceratops flies. Usually short waits. Height requirement: None

EPCOT: World Discovery

Frozen Ever After

Essential Tips for Parents

Rider Switch is Your Friend

If you have kids of different ages, use Rider Switch. One parent rides with the older kid while the other waits with the toddler, then you swap without waiting again.

Best Times for Toddlers

Early morning (rope drop) and late evening (after dinner) have the shortest lines. Toddlers do best when it's not packed and hot.

Don't Skip the Breaks

Toddlers need downtime. Plan for a midday break at your hotel, or find a quiet spot in the park. The Baby Care Centers are lifesavers.

Pack Smart

Bring snacks, a change of clothes, and their comfort item. Disney is magical but overstimulatingâ€”familiar items help.

Ready to Plan Your Trip?

Disney World with a toddler is exhausting but unforgettable. Focus on these gentle rides, take breaks, and don't try to do everything. The memories you'll make are worth every moment.

Questions About Toddler Rides?

Q: Can my 18-month-old go on any rides?
A: Absolutely! Most rides in Fantasyland have no height requirement and are perfect for 18-month-olds. "it's a small world," Winnie the Pooh, and Peter Pan are all gentle options. Just hold them securely on any ride with mild movement.
Q: What if my toddler gets scared on a ride?
A: Cast Members are trained to help. If your child gets scared before the ride starts, let them know and they can help you exit. If it's during the ride, comfort them and rememberâ€”you know your child best. There's no shame in skipping a ride if they're not ready.
