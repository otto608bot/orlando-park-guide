Best Shows at Disney World (Ranked)

From Broadway-quality productions to nighttime spectaculars, these are the Disney World shows that deserve a spot on your itinerary.

Disney World isn't just about rides—some of the most magical moments happen in theaters, amphitheaters, and even on the streets. After dozens of visits with my family, I've seen every show multiple times. Here's my honest ranking of the best shows at Disney World, from must-see to skip-if-you're-short-on-time.

The Top 5 Disney World Shows

1 Festival of the Lion King

📍 Animal Kingdom | ⏱️ 40 minutes

This is the gold standard of Disney World entertainment. A Broadway-caliber celebration of The Lion King featuring acrobats, dancers, singers, and those incredible Tumble Monkeys. The theater-in-the-round setup means there's no bad seat.

Parent tip: Arrive 20 minutes early for better seating. The show runs continuously throughout the day, so check the schedule and plan around it.

2 Fantasmic!

📍 Hollywood Studios | ⏱️ 30 minutes

Mickey's imagination comes to life in this nighttime spectacular on the lagoon. Fireworks, water projections, live performers, and a massive dragon that will make your jaw drop. It's the perfect end to a Hollywood Studios day.

Parent tip: Book the Fantasmic! Dining Package for guaranteed seating without the long wait. The Hollywood Brown Derby package is worth every penny.

3 Happily Ever After

📍 Magic Kingdom | ⏱️ 18 minutes

The fireworks show that replaced Wishes is pure Disney magic. Projections on Cinderella Castle, an incredible soundtrack, and enough pyrotechnics to light up the entire park. It's emotional, spectacular, and the perfect way to end your Magic Kingdom day.

Parent tip: Watch from Main Street for the full castle projection effect, or book a Fireworks Dessert Party for a reserved viewing spot with treats.

4 Indiana Jones Epic Stunt Spectacular

📍 Hollywood Studios | ⏱️ 30 minutes

Watch Indy fight Nazis, escape traps, and perform death-defying stunts—all while learning how movie magic happens. It's entertaining and educational, with audience participation that kids love.

Parent tip: Volunteer early if you want to be part of the show! The "extras" selected get a behind-the-scenes experience they won't forget.

5 Finding Nemo: The Big Blue... and Beyond!

📍 Animal Kingdom | ⏱️ 25 minutes

This puppetry masterpiece brings Nemo's underwater world to life with incredible costumes, puppeteers who become part of the performance, and a heartwarming story. The updated version adds new songs and scenes from Finding Dory.

Parent tip: Great for toddlers who might be scared by loud fireworks shows. The theater is also air-conditioned—a blessing on hot Florida days.

Honorable Mentions Worth Your Time

  • Beauty and the Beast – Live on Stage: A 25-minute Broadway-style adaptation at Hollywood Studios. Great for Belle fans.
  • Mickey and the Magical Map: A fun stage show at Magic Kingdom's Fantasyland Theatre featuring multiple Disney stories.
  • Disney Enchantment: Magic Kingdom's alternative fireworks show (check schedule—sometimes runs instead of Happily Ever After).
  • Tree of Life Awakenings: Short nighttime projections on Animal Kingdom's iconic tree. No wait, just walk up and watch.

Shows You Can Skip (Unless You Have Time)

  • Disney's Imagination! Pavilion shows: Dated and often empty.
  • The American Adventure: 30 minutes of animatronic history—great for a nap, but kids get restless.
  • Lightning McQueen's Racing Academy: Cute for Cars fans, but very short and repetitive.
Parent tip: Download the My Disney Experience app and check showtimes the morning of your visit. Schedules change seasonally, and some shows only run on select days.

Questions About Disney World Shows?

Ask below and we'll answer. Your question might help other parents too!

Q: What's the best show for toddlers who get scared easily?
A: Finding Nemo: The Big Blue... and Beyond! is perfect—no loud noises or scary moments, just colorful puppets and a gentle story. Skip Fantasmic! and the fireworks if your little one is sensitive to loud sounds.
Q: Do I need to book shows in advance?
A: Most shows don't require reservations—just show up. However, the Fantasmic! Dining Package is worth booking 60 days ahead for guaranteed good seats. For regular Fantasmic! viewing, arrive 45-60 minutes early.
