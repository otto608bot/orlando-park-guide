Can't decide between Walt Disney World and Universal Orlando? Our honest comparison covers rides, costs, ages, and more to help you choose the perfect park.

Best for Different Ages

Toddlers & Preschoolers (Ages 2-5)

Winner: Disney World

With over 40 attractions that have no height requirements, toddlers can experience the magic alongside the whole family. Highlights include Dumbo, It's a Small World, and Winnie the Pooh.

Elementary School Kids (Ages 6-11)

Winner: Tie (depends on interests)

Choose Disney if: Your kids love princesses, Star Wars, and gentle storytelling.

Choose Universal if: Your kids love Harry Potter, Nintendo, and slightly more thrilling adventures.

Tweens & Teens (Ages 12-17)

Winner: Universal Orlando

Universal pulls ahead significantly for this age group. The Wizarding World of Harry Potter feels genuinely magical, and thrill rides like Hagrid's Motorbike Adventure and VelociCoaster are must-dos.

Ride Intensity Comparison

Disney World: Gentle Immersion

Disney focuses on storytelling over thrills. Even their "scariest" rides are relatively tame. Most attractions have no height requirements and move slowly through immersive scenes.

Universal: Thrills and Adventure

Universal embraces intensity. Many headline attractions have 40-54 inch height requirements. The trade-off? More exciting experiences for older kids and adults.

Cost Comparison

Disney World: Generally more expensive for tickets, food, and hotels. The experience is more immersive but comes at a premium.

Universal Orlando: Often slightly less expensive, especially for shorter trips. Express Pass (skip-the-line) is more affordable than Disney's Lightning Lane.

The Verdict

Choose Disney World if: You have young kids, want classic character experiences, prefer gentle rides, or are looking for the most immersive themed environments.

Choose Universal if: You have tweens/teens, love Harry Potter or Nintendo, want more thrilling rides, or prefer a more "grown-up" theme park experience.

Can't decide? Many families do both! A split stay (3 days Disney, 2 days Universal) gives you the best of both worlds.
