Do you need a stroller for Disney World? If your child is under 6, the answer is probably yes. Even kids who haven't used a stroller in months will need one after walking 8-10 miles per day in the Florida heat.

But not just any stroller will do. Disney World has specific size restrictions, and you'll be folding it repeatedly for buses, monorails, and parking lot trams. The wrong stroller will make your vacation harder, not easier.

Disney World Stroller Rules (2026)

Before we get to recommendations, here are the current rules:

  • Size limit: Strollers must be 31" wide and 52" long or smaller
  • No wagon-style strollers (even pushable ones)
  • Stroller parking: Available outside most attractions
  • Rental option: $15/day for single, $31/day for double (multi-day discounts available)

Our advice? Bring your own if possible. Disney rental strollers are hard plastic, don't recline, and can't leave the parks. A good travel stroller is worth the investment.

Must-Have Stroller Accessories

The stroller is just the start. These accessories will make your park days significantly easier:

1. Stroller Fan ($20-30)

Florida heat is no joke. A rechargeable clip-on fan keeps air moving over your sleeping baby and helps prevent overheating. Get one with flexible legs so you can position it perfectly.

2. Stroller Hooks ($10-15)

Disney bags. Souvenir bags. Water bottles. Your stroller's storage basket will fill fast. Stroller hooks let you hang bags from the handlebar—just don't overload it or the stroller could tip.

3. Stroller Rain Cover ($15-25)

Afternoon thunderstorms are common in Orlando. A rain cover keeps your child dry during sudden downpours and blocks wind on chilly mornings.

4. Apple AirTag ($25-100)

You'll park your stroller in "stroller parking" areas outside attractions and restaurants. With thousands of similar strollers, AirTags help you find yours quickly. Hide one in the storage basket or under the seat.

Should You Rent a Stroller at Disney?

Disney offers stroller rentals, but we generally don't recommend them for multi-day trips:

  • Hard plastic seats – No padding, can't recline
  • Can't leave the parks – You'll be without a stroller at your resort
  • No storage – Barely room for a diaper bag
  • Expensive over time – $15/day adds up fast

Better option: Buy a lightweight travel stroller (like the MAMAZING Ultra Air) and bring it with you. You'll have it at the airport, resort, and parks. After your trip, keep it for future travel or sell it.

Final Tips for Stroller Success at Disney

  1. Label your stroller – Tie a ribbon or use a luggage tag. Strollers look alike.
  2. Don't leave valuables – Cast Members may move your stroller while you're on rides.
  3. Practice folding – You'll do it dozens of times. Make sure you can do it one-handed.
  4. Bring a backup – For toddlers right on the edge, consider a stroller fan and cooling towels to extend stroller use.
  5. Know when to quit – If your 5-year-old is fighting the stroller, let them walk. Every kid is different.

Have questions about strollers at Disney World? The right setup makes a huge difference in your vacation experience. Invest in a good travel stroller and the essential accessories—you won't regret it.
