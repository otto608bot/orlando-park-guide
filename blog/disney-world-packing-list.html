Disney World Packing List 2026: The Complete Guide

The ultimate packing list for your Disney World vacation. Essentials for adults, kids, toddlers, and babies—plus a free printable checklist.

💰 Save up to $50: Get discounted Disney tickets through Undercover Tourist →

Packing for Disney World isn't like packing for a typical vacation. Between the Florida heat, unpredictable rain, and 12-hour park days, you need to be strategic about what goes in your suitcase.

After dozens of Disney trips with my own family—and learning the hard way what we actually needed—I've compiled the ultimate packing list. This covers everything from park bag essentials to things you probably haven't thought of.

The Disney World Packing Philosophy

Before we dive into the list, here's the mindset: pack light, but pack smart. You'll be walking 8-12 miles per day, carrying bags through security, and navigating crowded parks. Every item should earn its place.

Parent tip: We keep a "park bag" that stays packed with essentials (sunscreen, portable charger, snacks) so we can grab it and go each morning. Game changer.

Park Bag Essentials

These items go in your daily park bag—the smaller bag you'll carry into the parks:

🎒 Daily Park Bag

Portable phone charger
Sunscreen (SPF 30+)
Refillable water bottle
Lightweight rain poncho
Hand sanitizer
Wet wipes
Snacks (granola bars, crackers)
Blister pads/Band-Aids
Pain relievers (Tylenol/Advil)
Sunglasses
Hat or visor
Disney MagicBand or ticket cards

Clothing Essentials

Florida weather is hot, humid, and prone to afternoon thunderstorms. Pack accordingly:

👕 Clothing

Moisture-wicking shirts (2 per day)
Comfortable shorts (1 per day)
Lightweight pants (1-2 pairs)
Light jacket or hoodie (for AC)
Swimsuit (resort pools)
Pajamas
Underwear (1.5x days)
Socks (2 per day)
Comfortable walking shoes (broken in!)
Sandals/water shoes
Light rain jacket
Disney-themed shirts (optional)
Parent tip: Pack one "nice" outfit for character dining or Disney Springs. Most of the time you'll be in park clothes, but it's nice to have options.

For Kids & Toddlers

Traveling with little ones requires extra planning. Here's what to add:

👶 Kids & Toddler Extras

Stroller (or rent at Disney)
Stroller fan
Stroller rain cover
Diapers/wipes (more than you think)
Change of clothes (2 per day)
Favorite snacks
Small toys/activities for lines
Glow sticks/light-up toys
Baby carrier (for infants)
Sippy cups/bottles
Kid-safe sunscreen
Autograph book & pen

What NOT to Pack

Just as important as what to bring—here's what to leave home:

  • New shoes – Break them in first or you'll regret it
  • Heavy jackets – Even in winter, Florida is mild during the day
  • Valuable jewelry – Easy to lose on rides
  • Large bags – You'll carry it all day; keep it minimal
  • Food for the whole trip – Grocery delivery to your resort is easier
  • Ponchos from home – Buy cheap ones at the dollar store or at Disney

The "Oh Yeah, That" List

The easily forgotten items that you'll definitely need:

📝 Don't Forget

ID/driver's license
Travel insurance documents
Medications (in original bottles)
Portable phone charger (seriously, bring two)
Car rental confirmation
Disney reservation confirmations
Cash for tips
Reusable shopping bag (for souvenirs)

Final Tips for Packing Success

  1. Roll, don't fold – Saves space and reduces wrinkles
  2. Pack outfits, not items – Plan what you'll wear each day
  3. Leave room for souvenirs – You'll buy more than you think
  4. Ship items ahead – Amazon can deliver to your Disney resort
  5. Pack a "first night" bag – Essentials in case luggage is delayed

Remember: you can buy almost anything at Disney World (for a premium). Don't stress if you forget something—focus on the essentials and the memories you'll make.
