    <h2>Spring Break Survival Strategies</h2>

    <h3>1. Arrive Before Rope Drop</h3>
    <p>During Spring Break, "rope drop" isn't early enough. Arrive at the park entrance 45-60 minutes before official opening. You'll be among the first inside and can knock out 2-3 major rides before the crowds arrive.</p>

    <div class="parent-tip">
      <strong>Parent tip:</strong> If you have early entry (Disney resort guests), arrive 30 minutes before that. You can ride Rise of the Resistance or Flight of Passage with minimal wait if you're first in line.
    </div>

    <h3>2. Buy Lightning Lane (Yes, It's Worth It)</h3>
    <p>During Spring Break, Lightning Lane isn't optional—it's essential. Without it, you'll wait 90+ minutes for headliners. With it, you can book Lightning Lanes and cut waits to 15-20 minutes. Budget $25-35 per person per day.</p>

    <h3>3. Take Midday Breaks</h3>
    <p>The parks are most crowded from 11 AM to 4 PM. Leave for a nap, pool time, or resort lunch. Return refreshed for evening when crowds thin slightly and the weather cools.</p>

    <h3>4. Book Dining 60 Days Out</h3>
    <p>Popular restaurants fill up fast during Spring Break. Set an alarm for 6 AM EST exactly 60 days before your trip. Priority bookings: Be Our Guest, Chef Mickey's, Ohana, and any character dining.</p>

    <h2>EPCOT Flower & Garden Festival</h2>

    <p>Spring Break coincides with EPCOT's International Flower & Garden Festival (runs through early July 2026). This is actually a great time to visit EPCOT because:</p>

    <ul>
      <li>The park is beautifully decorated with topiaries and gardens</li>
      <li>Outdoor kitchens offer unique food options that spread out the crowds</li>
      <li>The Garden Rocks concert series provides evening entertainment</li>
      <li>Spike's Pollen-Nation Exploration is a fun scavenger hunt for kids</li>
    </ul>

    <div class="parent-tip">
      <strong>Parent tip:</strong> The festival kitchens can be pricey, but they're a great way to try small portions without committing to a full meal. Budget $8-12 per item.
    </div>

    <h2>Best Parks During Spring Break</h2>

    <p><strong>Animal Kingdom:</strong> Often the least crowded because it has fewer rides. Perfect for Spring Break—ride Flight of Passage early, then enjoy the shows and animals.</p>

    <p><strong>EPCOT:>/strong> The festival helps distribute crowds, and World Showcase is great for walking and exploring even when busy.</p>

    <p><strong>Hollywood Studios:</strong> Expect 2+ hour waits for Rise of the Resistance and Slinky Dog Dash. Get Lightning Lane and prioritize these.</p>

    <p><strong>Magic Kingdom:</strong> The most crowded park, but also has the most rides. Use Lightning Lane strategically and stay for fireworks.</p>
