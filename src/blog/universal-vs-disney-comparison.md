    <h2>Best for Different Ages</h2>

    <h3>Toddlers & Preschoolers (Ages 2-5)</h3>
    <p><strong>Winner: Disney World</strong></p>
    <p>With over 40 attractions that have <strong>no height requirements</strong>, toddlers can experience the magic alongside the whole family. Highlights include Dumbo, It's a Small World, and Winnie the Pooh.</p>

    <h3>Elementary School Kids (Ages 6-11)</h3>
    <p><strong>Winner: Tie (depends on interests)</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Choose Disney if:</strong> Your kids love princesses, Star Wars, and gentle storytelling.</p>
    <p><strong>Choose Universal if:</strong> Your kids love Harry Potter, Nintendo, and slightly more thrilling adventures.</p>

    <h3>Tweens & Teens (Ages 12-17)</h3>
    <p><strong>Winner: Universal Orlando</strong></p>
    <p>Universal pulls ahead significantly for this age group. The Wizarding World of Harry Potter feels genuinely magical, and thrill rides like Hagrid's Motorbike Adventure and VelociCoaster are must-dos.</p>

    <h2>Ride Intensity Comparison</h2>

    <h3>Disney World: Gentle Immersion</h3>
    <p>Disney focuses on <strong>storytelling over thrills</strong>. Even their "scariest" rides are relatively tame. Most attractions have no height requirements and move slowly through immersive scenes.</p>

    <h3>Universal: Thrills and Adventure</h3>
    <p>Universal embraces <strong>intensity</strong>. Many headline attractions have 40-54 inch height requirements. The trade-off? More exciting experiences for older kids and adults.</p>

    <h2>Cost Comparison</h2>

    <p><strong>Disney World:</strong> Generally more expensive for tickets, food, and hotels. The experience is more immersive but comes at a premium.</p>

    <p><strong>Universal Orlando:</strong> Often slightly less expensive, especially for shorter trips. Express Pass (skip-the-line) is more affordable than Disney's Lightning Lane.</p>

    <h2>The Verdict</h2>

    <p><strong>Choose Disney World if:</strong> You have young kids, want classic character experiences, prefer gentle rides, or are looking for the most immersive themed environments.</p>

    <p><strong>Choose Universal if:</strong> You have tweens/teens, love Harry Potter or Nintendo, want more thrilling rides, or prefer a more "grown-up" theme park experience.</p>

    <p><strong>Can't decide?</strong> Many families do both! A split stay (3 days Disney, 2 days Universal) gives you the best of both worlds.</p>
