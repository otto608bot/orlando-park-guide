    <h2>The Best Character Dining Experiences (Ranked)</h2>

    <h3>1. Topolino's Terrace — Best Overall Value</h3>
    
    <div class="restaurant-card">
      <h3>🎨 Topolino's Terrace at Disney's Riviera Resort</h3>
      <div class="restaurant-meta">
        <span>📍 Riviera Resort (Skyliner accessible)</span>
        <span>🍳 Breakfast only</span>
        <span>💰 $45/adult, $29/child</span>
      </div>
      <p><strong>Characters:</strong> Mickey (painter), Minnie (poet), Donald (sculptor), Daisy (dancer)</p>
      <p><strong>The food:</strong> This is hands-down the best character dining food at Disney World. We're talking sour cream waffles, wood-fired butcher's steak, and quiche Gruyère—not the typical buffet scramble.</p>
      <p><strong>Why it wins:</strong> The Riviera Resort setting is stunning, the characters wear adorable artist outfits, and the food is legitimately good. It's also one of the few character meals where adults won't feel like they're overpaying for mediocre buffet food.</p>
      <p><strong>Parent tip:</strong> Book the earliest breakfast slot (7:30 AM) and you can be done by 9:00 AM—perfect for rope-dropping Hollywood Studios or EPCOT.</p>
    </div>

    <h3>2. Chef Mickey's — The Classic Experience</h3>
    
    <div class="restaurant-card">
      <h3>🎉 Chef Mickey's at Disney's Contemporary Resort</h3>
      <div class="restaurant-meta">
        <span>📍 Contemporary Resort (walk from Magic Kingdom)</span>
        <span>🍳 Breakfast &amp; Dinner</span>
        <span>💰 $55/adult, $36/child</span>
      </div>
      <p><strong>Characters:</strong> Mickey, Minnie, Donald, Goofy, Pluto (the "Fab Five")</p>
      <p><strong>The experience:</strong> This is the character dining experience everyone pictures—classic characters, the monorail gliding overhead, and that iconic "Celebrate" song with napkin twirling. It's loud, chaotic, and exactly what kids dream of.</p>
      <p><strong>The food:</strong> Standard breakfast buffet (Mickey waffles, scrambled eggs, bacon) and dinner buffet with carved meats. Nothing special, but kids don't care.</p>
      <p><strong>Parent tip:</strong> Request a table near the windows for views of Bay Lake and the monorail. Dinner is significantly more expensive than breakfast with the same characters—go for breakfast.</p>
    </div>

    <h3>3. Cinderella's Royal Table — The Splurge</h3>
    
    <div class="restaurant-card">
      <h3>👑 Cinderella's Royal Table at Magic Kingdom</h3>
      <div class="restaurant-meta">
        <span>📍 Inside Cinderella Castle</span>
        <span>🍳 Breakfast, Lunch &amp; Dinner</span>
        <span>💰 $79/adult, $47/child</span>
      </div>
      <p><strong>Characters:</strong> Cinderella (greeting), plus rotating princesses like Ariel, Aurora, Jasmine, Snow White</p>
      <p><strong>The experience:</strong> Eating inside Cinderella Castle is bucket-list stuff for Disney families. The gothic architecture, stained glass windows, and princess meet-and-greets make this feel genuinely magical.</p>
      <p><strong>The food:</strong> Surprisingly decent for a character meal—think beef tenderloin, pan-seared chicken, and the famous "Clock Strikes Twelve" dessert. You're paying for the location, but at least the food won't disappoint.</p>
      <p><strong>Parent tip:</strong> This books up 60 days out. Set an alarm for 6:00 AM EST exactly 60 days before your trip. If you have a princess-obsessed kid, this is worth the splurge once. For subsequent trips, skip it.</p>
    </div>

    <h3>4. Tusker House — Best for Adventurous Families</h3>
    
    <div class="restaurant-card">
      <h3>🦁 Tusker House at Animal Kingdom</h3>
      <div class="restaurant-meta">
        <span>📍 Africa, Animal Kingdom</span>
        <span>🍳 Breakfast, Lunch &amp; Dinner</span>
        <span>💰 $47/adult, $30/child</span>
      </div>
      <p><strong>Characters:</strong> Donald, Daisy, Mickey, Goofy (in safari outfits)</p>
      <p><strong>The food:</strong> African-inspired cuisine alongside American standards. Think tandoori chicken, peri-peri salmon, and curries plus roasted meats and salads. It's the most interesting buffet at Disney World.</p>
      <p><strong>Why parents love it:</strong> The food is genuinely good, the theming is immersive, and it's a nice break from chicken fingers and pizza. Kids get their character fix while adults eat something with actual flavor.</p>
      <p><strong>Parent tip:</strong> Book lunch or dinner for the full African menu. Breakfast is more standard American fare. The restaurant is indoors and air-conditioned—a lifesaver on hot Animal Kingdom days.</p>
    </div>

    <h3>5. Hollywood &amp; Vine — Best for Toddlers</h3>
    
    <div class="restaurant-card">
      <h3>📺 Hollywood &amp; Vine at Hollywood Studios</h3>
      <div class="restaurant-meta">
        <span>📍 Hollywood Studios (near Echo Lake)</span>
        <span>🍳 Breakfast &amp; Lunch (Disney Jr.), Dinner (seasonal)</span>
        <span>💰 $47/adult, $30/child</span>
      </div>
      <p><strong>Characters (breakfast/lunch):</strong> Vampirina, Doc McStuffins, Fancy Nancy, Roadster Goofy</p>
      <p><strong>The experience:</strong> If you have a toddler obsessed with Disney Junior shows, this is non-negotiable. These characters don't meet elsewhere in the parks, so this is your only shot.</p>
      <p><strong>The food:</strong> Standard buffet fare. The "Play 'n Dine" breakfast has Mickey waffles and kid-friendly options. Nothing memorable for adults.</p>
      <p><strong>Parent tip:</strong> This is a must-do if your kid is 2-5 and watches Disney Junior. For older kids who've moved on to Marvel or Star Wars, skip it. The characters rotate based on show popularity—check current lineup before booking.</p>
    </div>

    <h2>Honorable Mentions</h2>

    <h3>Crystal Palace (Magic Kingdom)</h3>
    <p>Winnie the Pooh, Tigger, Piglet, and Eeyore in a beautiful Victorian greenhouse setting. The buffet is standard but the character interactions are sweet and unhurried. Great for young kids who love Pooh. ($47/adult)</p>

    <h3>Garden Grill (EPCOT)</h3>
    <p>A rotating restaurant with Chip 'n' Dale, Pluto, and Mickey in farmer outfits. Family-style service (no buffet) with fresh ingredients from The Land pavilion. Unique experience but limited character variety. ($47/adult)</p>

    <h3>Ohana (Polynesian Resort)</h3>
    <p>Family-style Hawaiian feast with Lilo, Stitch, Mickey, and Pluto at breakfast. Dinner has no characters—don't mix them up. The food is excellent (bread pudding!), but character time is brief. ($55/adult)</p>

    <h3>1900 Park Fare (Grand Floridian)</h3>
    <p>Recently reopened with an Alice in Wonderland theme. The Mad Hatter, Alice, and Tweedle Dee/Dum make this unique, but reviews are mixed on the new format. Check recent reviews before booking. ($54/adult)</p>

    <h2>Character Dining Strategy: What I Wish I Knew</h2>

    <div class="tips-box">
      <h3>💡 Pro Tips for Character Dining Success</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Book at 60 days:</strong> Popular spots like Cinderella's Royal Table and Topolino's fill up fast. Set an alarm for 6:00 AM EST.</li>
        <li><strong>Breakfast is cheaper:</strong> Same characters, lower prices. Chef Mickey's breakfast is $15 less than dinner.</li>
        <li><strong>Factor in travel time:</strong> Resort restaurants (Chef Mickey's, Ohana, Topolino's) require 45+ minutes of transit. Don't book during prime park time.</li>
        <li><strong>Bring autograph books:</strong> Characters will sign them. Sharpies work better than pens.</li>
        <li><strong>Don't over-schedule:</strong> Character meals take 60-90 minutes. One per day is plenty.</li>
        <li><strong>Check for dining packages:</strong> Some restaurants offer reserved fireworks viewing—worth it for special occasions.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <h2>Is Character Dining Worth It?</h2>

    <p>At $45-80 per adult, character dining isn't cheap. But when you break it down:</p>

    <ul>
      <li>Individual character meet-and-greets: 15-45 minutes of waiting per character</li>
      <li>Character dining: 4-5 characters in 60-90 minutes while you eat</li>
    </ul>

    <p>For families with young kids, character dining saves hours of queue time and guarantees face time with the characters they care about most. It's not just a meal—it's a time-management strategy.</p>

    <p><strong>My recommendation:</strong> Book one or two character meals per trip. Pick based on your kids' current obsessions (princesses, Disney Junior, classic characters) and balance cost with experience quality. Topolino's for food, Chef Mickey's for classic Disney magic, and Cinderella's Royal Table if you have a princess fan.</p>

    <p>The rest of your meals? Quick service and snacks. Save the sit-down dining budget for experiences that deliver both food and magic.</p>
